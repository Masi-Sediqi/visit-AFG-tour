Travel accessibility

  1. Dubai strives to enhance its offerings for travellers with special needs

    As a leading global destination, Dubai aims to improve travel accessibility for visitors with special needs, known as ‘People of Determination’ in the UAE. Whether it is at the airport, public transport, top attractions or accommodation, we continue to enhance the traveller experience to help you navigate our city with ease.

Airport Accessibility Guide

At Hamid Karzai International Airport (KBL) in Kabul and other major airports, travelers with disabilities can access essential services including wheelchair support, priority lanes, and immigration assistance. Accessible washrooms, dedicated parking, and assistance for visual or hearing impairments are also available. People with hidden disabilities, including autism, are encouraged to notify their airline in advance to ensure personalized support and smoother airport experiences.

Cruise Terminals

Afghanistan is landlocked and does not have cruise terminals. However, visitors can explore regional travel hubs in neighboring countries for cruise tourism.

Accommodation

Hotels in Afghanistan’s major cities offer a range of options. Some upscale hotels provide accessible rooms, ramps, and elevator access. Contact the property in advance to confirm availability of accessibility features.

Sights and Attractions

While many historical sites in Afghanistan are not fully accessible, some museums and modern public areas in cities like Kabul are working to accommodate visitors with physical limitations. It’s best to check ahead when planning your visit.

Fitness and Activities for People of Determination

Afghanistan is steadily developing inclusive spaces for physical activity. While not as advanced as global hubs, initiatives are underway to support fitness opportunities for people with disabilities. Parks, local NGOs, and a growing number of sports centers are offering basic accessible facilities in Kabul and other urban centers.

In global cities like Dubai, Paralympic gold medallist Kevin Paul showcases how accessibility is prioritized across fitness spaces such as Wellfit Gym, Aventura Parks, and Jumeirah Public Beach. These serve as inspiring models for cities like Kabul working toward more inclusive environments.

Accessibility FAQs for Afghanistan

Accessibility in Afghanistan is limited but gradually improving. In major cities like Kabul, some hospitals, educational institutions, and NGOs are working toward greater inclusion for people with disabilities. However, most public infrastructure remains a challenge.

At Hamid Karzai International Airport (Kabul), basic assistance is available upon request, such as wheelchair support and priority processing for travelers with physical limitations. However, services may vary and it's strongly recommended to inform your airline in advance.

Some major hotels in Kabul and Herat may offer limited accessibility features such as elevator access and wide entryways. It is important to contact the hotel ahead of time to confirm available facilities for guests with disabilities.

Public parks and recreational areas in Afghanistan are generally not fully accessible. Some new developments may have ramps or accessible pathways, but these are rare. Awareness and infrastructure are still developing.

Public transportation is very limited for people with disabilities. Taxis and buses usually lack accessibility features. Some private organizations and NGOs offer transport services, but availability is limited and must be arranged in advance.
